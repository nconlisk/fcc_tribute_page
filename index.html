Nikola Tesla

Noteworthy facts from Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born on the 10 July in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).
  • 1884 - Emigrated to the United States to take up a role at the Edison Machine Works, New York.
  • 1889 - Becoming independently weathly off the back of his AC patents. Tesla spent the next dozen years working out of a series of workshops and lab spaces in Manhatten. Some of Tesla's most significant works were conducted in the following Manhatten labs: 175 Grand Street, the fourth floor of 33-35 South Fifth Avenue, and the 6th and 7th floors of 46 & 48 East Houston Street.
  • 1891- On July 30th, aged 35, Tesla became a naturalised citizen of the United States. This was also the same year he patented his famous Tesla Coil.
  • Sleep - It is said that Tesla never slept more than 2hrs a night. He was know for working long periods without any rest, with one report claiming he once worked for a period of 84hrs straight.
  • Eidetic Memory - Tesla supposedly possessed a photographic memory, memorising complete textbooks and claimed to often fully visualise the intricate details of a new invention before moving on to the construction phase.
  • Patents - Over 300 obtained worldwide for his inventions.
  • 1943 - Died on the 7 January, aged 86, in New York City, United States.
